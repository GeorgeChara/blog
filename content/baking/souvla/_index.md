<h1>Souvla</h1>
<p style="color: #888; margin-top: -0.5em;">chicken thighs on charcoal, 45-60 min</p>

## Ingredients

<pre style="padding: 1em; border-radius: 4px; display: inline-block; margin: 0; color: #000;"><span style="color: #888;">Serves 4</span>

chicken thighs   8
olive oil        60g
lemon juice      50g
salt             10g
black pepper     4g
ground cumin     3g
</pre>

<span style="display:block; color:#888; font-size:0.8em; margin-top:0.8em; border-left: 2px solid #E5DECF; padding-left: 0.6em;">Bone-in, skin-on thighs. Cheap, juicy and the skin crisps up properly over charcoal.<br>Skewer across the bone, not through it lengthwise. This keeps the meat secure without splitting it.<br><strong>Big upgrade:</strong> swap the olive oil for buttermilk. Tenderises the meat deeply and gives a better crust when it chars. Marinate overnight if using buttermilk.</span>

## Recipe

**1.** Mix the olive oil (or buttermilk), lemon juice, salt, pepper and cumin in a bowl. Add the chicken thighs and coat well. Marinate for at least 2 hours, overnight is better.

**2.** Light the charcoal 1 hour before you want to cook. Wait until it is at the white ash stage before putting the meat on.

**3.** Skewer the thighs across the bone, not through it lengthwise. Push the skewer through the thickest part of the meat perpendicular to the bone so it sits flat and secure.

**4.** Start the skewers high above the coals to sear the outside and seal the juices. After 10 minutes lower closer to the heat.

**5.** Cook for 45-60 minutes total, turning every few minutes. Brush with any leftover marinade as you go.

**6.** The chicken is done when the skin is deep golden and crispy, the juices run clear and an internal temperature of 75°C is reached. The meat should pull away from the bone easily.

<span style="display:block; color:#888; font-size:0.8em; margin-top:1.2em; border-left: 2px solid #E5DECF; padding-left: 0.6em;"><strong>Swap ins and additions:</strong><br>Lemon zest in the marinade adds more citrus depth than juice alone.<br>Lemon pepper instead of black pepper and cumin for a sharper, cleaner heat.<br>White pepper instead of black for a softer heat that does not compete with the lemon.</span>
